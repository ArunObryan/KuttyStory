Chapter One

The Moment

One day he was sitting on a bench in a quiet park. There was a lake in front of him, surrounded by lots of Sakura trees. It was spring season and the trees were filled with pink flowers. A cold breeze blew from his right to left. It was a shower of pink flowers on him—calm, happy, like a blessing.

He heard a giggle of a child, quite some distance to his right. He turned and saw two kids enjoying this shower of flowers. It was a wholesome moment. As the kids were jumping and shouting, one man gave his hand to the short cute one. The little one jumped and grabbed his giant index finger, which fit into her whole palm.

The man was in a full black-grey long coat, well dressed and groomed. He looked fit, in his mid-thirties, with broad shoulders and a muscular lean body. He looked down to his left and started moving forward. The big one took the hand of a lady in a beige coat with a hat—a classy lady with a big bright smile. She looked down, talking to her big boy kid who wanted to play there a while longer. It felt like they were getting late for somewhere and wanted to go fast. So all four started walking together, with the breeze showering flowers on them. A dream moment for the man sitting there, observing this beautiful family.

He started thinking: if there was a chance to have this kind of moment in his life, with whom would it be? The question arose in his mind. On the other half of his brain, the answer was already there. It didn't take much time for the face to come to his mind. Chapter Two

Who was that person ?

Bring back memories

There was a woman he met at a certain point in his life. When they first started talking, everything felt easy and natural. It was a calm and beautiful phase of life, where conversations flowed without effort. They spent time together, played together, and shared little stories about their lives. Many days passed with simple moments—having lunch, sharing dinner, and sometimes spending time with friends.

She trusted him and slowly opened up about her life, sharing memories and experiences that mattered to her. He listened, and he liked those moments. There were also phases when the world around them felt quiet. Their friends had moved to different places, and there were days when it was just the two of them spending time together. In those moments, time seemed slower and simpler.

As time went on, new friends entered their lives and new groups began to form. Among all the things they did together, there was one thing they both always said yes to—playing badminton with their friends. It became a small tradition, a simple joy they never turned down.

But as life moved forward, things slowly began to change. Their circles grew, and the groups of friends around them became different. What once felt like a small shared world gradually expanded into separate paths, each surrounded by new people and new moments.

Chapter Three

How did he meet this person ?

It was a fine evening. The sunlight slipped through the window and gently filled his room, waking him from his sleep. His mind was empty, peaceful—he felt like a completely free man. But he was also a hungry man.

He went downstairs to meet his friends and grab something to eat. They sat together chatting and enjoying simple street food—kachori and pav bhaji. After finishing, they began walking back toward his place, laughing and talking along the way.

As they walked, they noticed a small group of people nearby calling out to one of his friends. So they stopped and joined the conversation.

Among that group, he noticed a girl standing slightly turned away. A moment later, she turned back and glanced at him.

Her eyes were beautiful, gently defined with a touch of mascara. A small black bindi sat perfectly between her eyebrows. It was a frame he felt he could look at all day. When she looked down, a bright smile appeared—her teeth shining behind a set of braces.

Her hair was neatly groomed, and her dressing had a quiet elegance.

For a brief moment, everything else faded into the background.

And somewhere in the distance, it almost felt like Anirudh was singing softly—"So baby, don't you break, break my… break my heart…"

Chapter Five

Stages of Life

What he manifested with her ?

In my journey through life, I have seen different phases of love. Each phase asks for a different kind of love. It begins with being someone's best friend, then a lover, then a partner. Later, it grows into being parents, and eventually grandparents. And the journey goes on.

Each phase asks for a little more effort than the one before it. Yet each stage has its own charm, where love is expressed through different actions.

During the dating phase, love feels light and exciting. We spend time together, roam around the city, travel, party with friends, and talk for hours without noticing time pass. In those moments, love feels simple and joyful.

Then comes the phase of marriage. Love begins to take a deeper form. It is seen in the way one cares for the other's family, respects every person who matters to them, and makes space for them in their life—even when their partner is not around. Love quietly expands beyond just two people and begins to include the entire family. It grows larger, expressed through responsibility and care.

Then comes the phase of motherhood. Love becomes even more gentle and protective. A man may show his love by massaging her tired feet, patiently handling her mood swings, and simply being there beside her as they prepare to bring a child into the world. She becomes a mother—perhaps a cute one who can curl up beside the baby in the crib and fall asleep next to them.

And life continues to unfold. Each new phase asks for more energy, more patience, and more understanding. There may be days when one of us feels tired, when life feels heavy, and the other must take that extra step to keep things moving forward.

Chapter Seven

Vaalvin Azhagiyal

There were moments that quietly defined their relationship. Small moments, yet deeply meaningful.

Like the time she stayed at the hospital with her mother, lying beside her on the same bed. Even in that difficult time, there was warmth in the way they cared for each other. Her mother would call her every hour, checking in, speaking with the same affection one would show to family. Slowly, it felt like she too had become a part of their little circle of friends. She shared her happy moments with them, and they shared theirs with her.

To him, it felt almost like a dream. It was everything he had imagined a family to be—people appreciating the smallest moments, supporting one another, and encouraging each other through life. And he had the chance to be there, witnessing those moments.

They had traveled far together and gone through many experiences along the way. Through all of it, he kept believing in something simple—the quiet signs the universe leaves behind.

If something is truly meant for you, it will remain in your life until the moment you are ready for it. Of course, it also asks something from you. It asks you to grow, to become worthy of the love you hope to receive.

Maybe, he thought, the universe was giving them this time to understand each other better—to spend time together now so that someday they could live a lifetime filled with happiness, love, and laughter.

He imagined a future where they would live in a city where their dreams and passions slowly turned into reality. A life surrounded by friends and family who genuinely wanted them to be happy and successful.

He had also witnessed beautiful relationships around them—people they both knew personally. He had seen how those couples carried their relationships through different phases of life, learning, adjusting, and growing together. Watching them gave him hope. It gave him the courage to ask these questions in his own heart.

Over time, he had learned something important about relationships.

You keep learning, every single day. And that is the beauty of it.

Sometimes he was amazed by friends who carried long-distance relationships for years, holding on to their commitment despite distance and time. Those stories made him believe in love even more.

Because when two people spend enough time together and are genuine with each other, love begins to feel simple and natural. Of course, there will be disagreements. There will be moments of frustration. But what matters is respect—the willingness to listen and the patience to allow the other person to speak their heart.

Effort matters the most in a relationship. And effort is not always fifty-fifty. Sometimes one person carries more, and sometimes the other does.

Listening to your partner might be the most romantic thing in a relationship. And sometimes the one who talks a lot—the playful yapper—ends up making life more interesting.

He felt he could put in that effort. And strangely, it never felt like effort when it brought a smile to her face.

And then, with a small smile to himself, he thought of one more philosophical line:

It is not about who likes you or who shows interest in you. It is about who is brave enough to take the risk of pursuing you—intentionally and consistently. Someone who chooses to keep showing up, whose actions quietly match their words.

He paused there for a moment.

Because that part of the story…

Only she could tell.
